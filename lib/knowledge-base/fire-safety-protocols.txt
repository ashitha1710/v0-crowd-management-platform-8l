# Fire Safety Protocols for Crowded Events

## Fire Prevention Measures

### Venue Preparation
- All exit doors must remain unlocked and unobstructed at all times
- Emergency exit signs must be illuminated and visible
- Fire lanes must be kept clear for emergency vehicles
- Electrical equipment must be UL-certified and regularly inspected
- Pyrotechnics and open flames strictly prohibited unless permitted
- Smoking designated to approved areas only
- Extension cords and multi-plug adapters limited to prevent overloading

### Crowd Management for Fire Safety
- Maximum capacity signs posted and enforced
- Trained venue staff monitoring capacity levels
- Emergency announcements system tested before event
- Crowd barriers placed to prevent blocking of exits
- Alcohol service limited to prevent impaired evacuation

## Fire Emergency Response Procedures

### Discovering Fire or Smoke
Immediate Actions:
1. Activate the nearest fire alarm pull station
2. Call emergency services (dial 911) and report location and nature of emergency
3. Alert security and event staff to initiate evacuation
4. If safe, attempt to extinguish small fires with appropriate extinguishers

### Evacuation Procedures
- Sound evacuation alarms throughout facility
- Announce "FIRE - EVACUATE IMMEDIATELY" using public address system
- Guide crowd to nearest emergency exits
- Assist elderly, disabled, and children first
- Prevent crowd surges at exit points
- Account for all staff and critical personnel

### Firefighting Priorities
- Protect human life first
- Evacuate the building
- Prevent fire spread to critical areas
- Contain fire until professionals arrive
- Do not re-enter building until declared safe by fire officials

## Fire Extinguisher Usage Guidelines

### Types of Fire Extinguishers
- Class A: Ordinary combustibles (wood, paper, cloth)
- Class B: Flammable liquids (gasoline, oil, grease)
- Class C: Electrical equipment
- Class D: Combustible metals
- Class K: Kitchen oils and greases

### PASS Method for Extinguisher Use
- Pull the pin
- Aim at base of fire
- Squeeze the lever
- Sweep side to side

### When NOT to Use Fire Extinguishers
- Fire too large to control quickly
- Fire spreading rapidly or jumping rooms
- Smoke too dense to see fire location
- Lack of safe escape route
- Extinguisher uncertain or unfamiliar

## Emergency Communication

### Key Emergency Phrases
- "Attention all patrons: There is a fire in the building. Please evacuate immediately using the nearest exit."
- "Security and staff: Fire emergency activated. Initiate evacuation procedures."
- "First responders: Fire located at [location]. Evacuation in progress."

### Communication Equipment
- Public address system (PA) - primary method
- Emergency intercom system
- Walkie-talkies for staff coordination
- Cellular phones for emergency services
- Emergency text alert system if available

## Coordinator Responsibilities During Fire

### Stage/Area Coordinators
- Know location of nearest fire extinguishers and alarms
- Be familiar with evacuation routes for assigned area
- Assist in crowd management during evacuation
- Report to command post once area is cleared

### Security Team
- Establish perimeters to prevent re-entry
- Guide emergency responders to fire location
- Prevent panic by providing clear directions
- Assist with crowd movement away from danger

### Medical Team
- Set up treatment areas away from building
- Assist with evacuation of injured or impaired individuals
- Monitor for smoke inhalation symptoms
- Coordinate with fire department for injured victims

## Post-Fire Procedures
- Do not re-enter building until permitted by fire officials
- Conduct head count and missing person searches
- Provide safety briefing to returning attendees
- Document incident and response effectiveness
